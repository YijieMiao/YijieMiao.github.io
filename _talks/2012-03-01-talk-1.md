In this talk, I shared an introductory overview of differential geometry and partial differential equations (PDEs), focusing on their deep interplay in modern mathematical research. I discussed how geometric intuition can guide the study of PDEs, using examples such as the Laplace-Beltrami operator and curvature-driven flows. The talk was intended for undergraduate students with a basic background in calculus and linear algebra, aiming to spark interest in these beautiful and powerful areas of mathematics.

This presentation was part of a student seminar series at the School of Mathematics, Southeast University.
